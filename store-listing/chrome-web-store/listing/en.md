SayThis lets you highlight unfamiliar text and hear the best available pronunciation source.

Use it for names, terms, abbreviations, and short phrases while preparing scripts, lessons, calls, or research notes. SayThis checks structured public sources, prefers verified recordings when available, and labels fallback speech paths clearly. Online lookup sends only the selected text or resolved pronunciation candidate to source services.

Useful for:

- unfamiliar names and places
- rare technical or academic terms
- abbreviations with unclear reading
- source forms written in another script

Features:

- context-menu and keyboard pronunciation actions
- popup lookup and playback controls
- verified recording choices when sources provide audio
- resolved source form, language, confidence, evidence, and source links
- optional lookup language hints for ambiguous text
- local correction, confirm, missing, and wrong-result feedback
- optional Forvo, custom source, place-name, and community endpoints
- no ads, analytics, or tracking

Browser voice availability varies by device. When SayThis cannot verify a matching voice for a non-English source form, it uses a simple guide when available or reports that speech is unavailable instead of playing a misleading fallback voice.
